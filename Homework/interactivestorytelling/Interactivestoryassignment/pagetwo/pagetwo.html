In spite of what these people say about these books, Dominic loved to read these books as he felt that the characters from the various tales found within these bound pages displayed values that had been long forgotten by his people. Among these values that he believed were lost long ago, Dominic believes the worst of these that were lost were one's sense of duty, honor, and compassion for other people. Secretly he wished that these values would one day return and start a new golden age for the people of the city, but was unsure as to what it would take to make it happen. One day, while walking through the marketplace, Dominic met a man there that he knew since childhood. The man’s name was Aiden Gillis, who was now one of the watchmen of the city recently assigned to the marketplace section of the city. They talked about old times, about how Aiden’s family was, but eventually the turned the conversation toward something that had happened the night before. He went on to tell Dominic about how last night one of the guards that were stationed on the northern watchtower, that over looked the food storage bunkers outside of the city, had fallen asleep during his watch. There was another guard, which was stationed at the bunkers, who deserted his post in order to go fishing with his friends. With no one on watch, the vaults were raided by bandits, who proceeded to empty most of the contents of the vaults before being driven off by one of the guards that had been patrolling the outer edge of the forest. The raid left barely enough food in the vaults for the people of the city to survive on for the next two weeks. Once the remaining food stuffs were gone, they would face starvation before the brutal winter to come unless their stores were restocked with food.
