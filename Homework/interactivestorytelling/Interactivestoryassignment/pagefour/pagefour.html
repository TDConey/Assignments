Sir Gawain of New Rapport

They're conversation was cut short when another guard came and told him that he had been ordered to report to the Mayor's office immediately. With an unsure look on his face, he bid Dominic farewell and walled toward the Mayor's office, which was stationed at the very center of their city. Dominic watched his friend, as he walked toward the Mayor's office away until he finally disappeared among the maze of buildings. He turned to walk back toward the city library, when he saw the Mayor's son sitting on top of a bench in the corner of the marketplace talking with his friends. As Dominic passed by him on the way back to work, he overheard the Mayor's son, the Mayor's nephew and their friends joking about the Captain's predicament, some even having the gall to say he should have gotten worse. He could feel his face flush with anger at the arrogance of these young men and their total lack of remorse over the chaos caused by their irresponsible behavior. If he were a violent man, these malcontents would get the thrashing that they deserved, but he hoped that one way or another they would get the punishment they had escaped. He had heard enough and walked angrily all the way back on the way to the library, almost knocking a few people over, as he passed by. In the back of his mind, he couldn't help but to think that he would have to return in the morning to witness the torture of this innocent man, because attendance was mandatory for all those that lived within the city walls. He opened the stain glass doors and stormed through the library to his office, where he threw himself down into the chair. There was a loud snapping sound, as he fell backward in his chair which caused his arms to be flung around in all directions trying to catch himself.
