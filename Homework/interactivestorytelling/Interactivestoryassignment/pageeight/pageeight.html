Pointing first to the Mayor's nephew, whose head was still stuck in the sewer drain and then to the Mayor's son, he told the crowd that these two were to blame. There were those in the crowd that cried out for an explanation, so the Captain told him all that transpired the night before and told him how the Mayor tried to place to blame on him for his son and nephew's failings. There were hurried conversations amongst those in the crowd, debating over what they had just heard. Captain Campbell had always been a well respected man, who had fought off all threats to the city valiantly and had always been a man of his word. This, along with the fact that the Mayor and his family were known for twisting the laws for their own selfish gains, turned the crowd against the Mayor. The angry eyes of the crowd fell upon the Mayor, who was still holding his son in his arms, as angry shouts began demanding the Captain's release. The guards walked away from the Mayor and over to the Captain, where they released him from his chains, as the crowd picked up the Mayor, the Mayor's son, and The Mayor's nephew and carried them to the Northern gate of the city. The large iron gates were thrown open as the crowd threw the three men out of the city and slammed the doors shut. A couple of days later, the Mayor's worst fears were realized as the city chose a new Mayor of Rapport, Eric Campbell! The city wanted to hold a grand celebration in the new Mayor's honor, but he declined and instead decided to sent out groups of volunteer to gather food to replace those lost in the raid. After the last group had left the safety of the city walls, Mayor Campbell went in search of the one person who was missing from the festivities.
